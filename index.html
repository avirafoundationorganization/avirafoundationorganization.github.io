OUR APPROACH


Vision

A society where all women, girls, trans, intersex, non-binary, and other gender-diverse people, especially those who are underprivileged, marginalized, and minorities, co-exist with dignity, self-respect, human rights, and equal opportunities. We believe that these individuals already possess inherent leadership and are empowered. We aim to further facilitate this by removing barriers and creating spaces that support them to take on leadership roles. By diminishing gender disparities, advocating for feminism, and initiating societal transformation, we strive to make empowerment accessible and nuanced, ensuring that their voices are heard and respected.

Mission

To support women, trans, intersex, non-binary, and other gender-diverse people in becoming self-reliant in livelihoods. We work toward bridging the gaps that hinder their access to rights, facilitating further knowledge and awareness building, and creating opportunities where they can advocate for themselves with various stakeholders within and outside of the community. We aim to facilitate their meaningful participation as citizens through training and promote their leadership in communities and beyond through Feminist Women's Leadership Groups, enabling self-reliance so they can claim and secure their rights as decision-makers, advocates, and leaders.

Goal

Our goals include supporting women, girls, trans, intersex, non-binary and other gender-diverse people's empowerment in achieving gender equality. In three years, we aim for 300 girls and gender-diverse youth in community leadership positions and connect 250 women and gender-diverse people with sustainable livelihoods. We will support 200 marginalized girls and gender-diverse youth in higher education and establish 10 grassroots libraries. Additionally, we strive to have 50 women and gender-diverse people start businesses, raise awareness about POCSO among 300 students, governance and facilitate legal and human rights awareness among 300 women and gender-diverse people.

About

Key milestones in our journey toward gender justice

  • April 2020

    Our Humble Beginnings

    The Avira Foundation began its journey with the aim of driving social change and addressing deep-rooted issues like gender inequality, lack of formal education, and bonded labor in Haryana.

  • August 2022

    Official Establishment

    Avira Foundation was officially established under the Trust Act of the Government of Haryana. This marked the formal beginning of its operations as a women and queer people-led grassroots organization dedicated to working with adolescent girls and young women.

  • August 2022

    Launch of Haryana Legal Network (HLN)

    Avira Foundation launched HLN to provide free legal assistance and address systemic exploitation and violence against marginalized individuals.

  • January 2023

    Expansion to 50 Villages

    Avira Foundation expanded its operations to 50 villages across five districts in Haryana: Sonipat, Panipat, Karnal, Jind, and Kaithal. This expansion aimed to tackle issues of violence, atrocities, and discrimination prevalent in these areas by amplifying narratives and working toward gender justice.

Haryana Legal Network

Supporting empowerment of marginalized women and gender-diverse people with free legal aid.

The Haryana Legal Network (HLN) by Avira Foundation supports women in vulnerable social positions and gender-diverse people facing systemic exploitation and violence with pro-bono legal assistance in Haryana. HLN collaborates with women lawyers from Dalit and other marginalized and/or minority communities, sensitizes young lawyers, and facilitates their capacity building to handle cases of gender-based violence (GBV). HLN aims to provide a holistic approach to legal support by offering free legal aid from lower courts to the Supreme Court, close monitoring of cases, as well as victim rehabilitation, promoting access to justice towards an equitable society.

Youth Leadership Training

Supporting young girls and gender-diverse young people in further building their leadership skills to drive social change.

Youth Leadership Training, spearheaded by the Avira Foundation, supports young girls and gender-diverse people in Haryana in developing their leadership skills further. This program focuses on education, capacity building, and policy understanding training, enabling them to develop and continue to take leadership roles within their communities and beyond. We define “training” not in its traditional definition, but in a holistic, all-encompassing sense and include self-defence training, emotional and psychosocial support, sports activities, exposure visits, film shows, theatre, and art workshops within our program. By fostering a sense of self-reliance and social responsibility, the training aims to support women and gender-diverse people in becoming leaders driving social change and promoting gender equality, thereby transforming not just their communities but also policies, initiatives, norms, and society at large.

People-Centered Support

Raising voices for justice and equality through organized action.

People-centred Support by the Avira Foundation mobilizes people from marginalized communities to collectively demand justice and equality. Through organized actions, the foundation addresses systemic issues such as gender-based violence and discrimination. Support efforts focus on educating the public and policymakers about systemic injustices toward women, girls, trans, intersex, non-binary and other gender-diverse people that our society is marred with, enabling the creation of a culture of equality and empowerment for all.

Legal Camps

Bringing essential legal knowledge and support to the school and college doorstep of marginalized and vulnerable communities.

Legal Camps, organized by the Avira Foundation, aim to support women from marginalized communities, as well as other gender-diverse people in Haryana with essential legal knowledge and care. These camps offer free legal consultations, awareness sessions on legal rights available to them under the Indian legal system, and aid and assistance in filing legal cases. By collaborating with experienced lawyers, the vamps support women and gender-diverse people to seek justice and address the systemic issue of gender-based violence (GBV) including domestic violence, land disputes, and caste-based atrocities. Through these initiatives, the foundation promotes legal awareness and strengthens community resilience.

Awareness Programs

Supporting the empowerment of communities & youth through legal and social awareness.

Awareness Camps by the Avira Foundation are designed to support youth from marginalized communities, as well as other gender-diverse people with further understanding of their rights and provide crucial legal and social awareness and information. These camps offer interactive sessions on topics such as gender equality, legal rights, and health awareness, supporting women and gender-diverse people to make informed decisions and take actions that flow through this information. By collaborating with local leaders and experts, the foundation ensures that the camps address issues that are truly relevant for the communities.

With Love, Resilience

Amplifying the voices of Dalit women and gender-diverse people for justice and equality.

With Love, Resilience is a campaign by the Avira Foundation that centers care and love at its core. It emphasizes how resistance to structural and systemic injustices stems from a profound place of love and care for what we are fighting for. To love is to resist. The campaign highlights narratives from various intersections—feminism, queer justice, the Dalit movement, and more—demonstrating how they interconnect. It aims to provide a platform for women, girls, trans, intersex, non-binary, and other gender-diverse people to express their love and resistance, sharing their collective resilience. Additionally, it creates spaces and opportunities for digital actions, fostering meaningful change.
